Jot — visual design comparison

Five distinct design directions for Jot's iOS main screen, each rendered as a browser-approximated iPhone mockup across four states: idle, recording, transcribing, and copied to clipboard. For the full rationale, HIG compliance table, micro-interactions catalog, and recommended paths forward, see visual-interface-mockups.md. The live SwiftUI source for each variant lives alongside this file as 1_Aperture.swift through 5_Dictaphone.swift. These HTML renders are static approximations — Material blur, SF Rounded, and spring animations are best-effort; the true feel is in Xcode Preview.

Tap the hairline drag-handle above the home-indicator (or swipe up) to summon a native .sheet with three .presentationDetents: small (12% peek · 3 recents), medium (42% · scrollable + search), large (.large · full archive with date sections). Same gesture users already know from Maps and Find My.

Aperture is the safest direction — it reads like a native Apple app by borrowing Voice Memos' vocabulary: a large SF Rounded timer, a mic button inside a hairline ring that doubles as the level meter, a quiet all-caps caption, and a gray transcript container with a Copy/Share row muted until there's a result. Pairs best with press-and-hold dictation (the ring becomes the hold-arc) and suffix commands (the caption hosts "Command detected — tightened"). HIG compliance: 10/10 — zero brand risk, and every v1 requirement from §1 of the brief is solved inside Apple's own vocabulary.

A serif Archive link in the nav bar's trailing position pushes a full-screen read-mode archive. Date mastheads in italic NY serif, numbered entries (Entry 42.), two-line previews in the same serif. Pull-down .searchable handles search. Scene-change instead of drawer keeps the literary calm.

Granola strips chrome to prose. A massive New York serif headline mirrors state ("Ready to listen." → "Listening…" → "Writing it down…" → "Here you go."), a single Material pill hosts the toggle and timer, and the transcript sits on the page as serif body text — no container, no frame. Pairs best with implicit routing (the headline becomes "Sending to Marcus…") and chained follow-ups (previous transcript fades to 40% and the new one cross-fades in). HIG compliance: 8/10 — deviates from Apple's preference for filled containers, justified by the app's promise of quiet.

Pulse hides the app chrome entirely — a floating island pill pinned below the Dynamic Island hosts the mic button, a waveform during recording, and a state label; transcripts accumulate as a reverse-chronological log of cards. Dark-OLED by default (the island metaphor only works on dark, and OLED iPhones save battery). Pairs best with chained follow-ups (the log is the chain) and press-and-hold (island color flips to amber for hold-commands). HIG compliance: 7/10 — dark-only deviates from "support both appearances," but every primitive used is Apple's own.

A 44pt-tall bottom strip (ARCHIVE · 0042 ENTRIES) pushes into a mono tape-deck log. Each row is #0042 · 09:02 · 00:18 (index · time · duration) on one mono header line; transcript body in SF Pro below. Date sections as full-width mono-tracked banners. Reads like a print-out from a studio logger.

NothingJot takes cues from Nothing OS, Teenage Engineering's OP-1, and Braun calculators — SF Mono everywhere, an 84pt mono timer, a 24-segment VU meter that climbs primary → orange → red, and a dot-matrix canvas in the background. The most memorable variant, and the most niche. Pairs best with suffix commands (the top strip could flash amber "SUFFIX ● CLEAN") but struggles with implicit routing — this aesthetic is "instrument," not "assistant." HIG compliance: 5/10 — deliberately non-Apple-shippable, best shipped as an opt-in "Workshop" theme rather than the default.

Tap book.closed in the masthead: main surface slides 72% right, revealing a cream sidebar of past entries from the leading edge. Italic serif date headers, numbered Entry #42 labels, two-line previews. Crimson rule marks the current entry. The notebook metaphor — turning a page, not a modal drawer from below.

Dictaphone trades semantic neutrality for one justified brand color — a warm cream paper tone with espresso ink and a deep crimson accent — and replaces amplitude bars with a single continuous scribble stroke that redraws on input. New York serif everywhere, SF Mono for the timer, underlined serif buttons in the footer. Pairs best with implicit routing ("To Marcus:") and chained follow-ups (previous transcript slides up into smaller "marginalia"). HIG compliance: 6/10 — the cream is a brand deviation, justified by the same precedent Apple uses in Journal, Books, and Apple TV+ literary pages.

Ledger is the 6th direction — NothingJot's instrument DNA (mono timer, VU meter, "operating-a-device" feel) grafted onto Pulse's bones (floating pill at the top, reverse-chronological log below, dark-first). Every Nothing-specific detail is deliberately replaced: horizontal ledger rules instead of dot-matrix; amber (warm CRT phosphor) instead of red; SF Mono instead of Ndot; pill-only chrome instead of the top-strip "JOT · 01" brand header. Numbered entries (#0041) frame the log as a running notebook rather than a chat feed. Pairs best with chained follow-ups (the ledger is the chain) and suffix commands (the status chip flips amber — CLEAN, EMAIL — without introducing a second hue). HIG compliance: 7/10 — dark-first, same asterisk as Pulse, but every primitive (Material, semantic system colors, SF Mono) is Apple's own.
